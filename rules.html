ERIDANUS

History Stars Questions

By: Andry Nasief

-This constellation was listed by Ptolemy

-It is the Six Largest of our modern 88 constellations

-It is in the Southern Celestial Hemisphere.

-It was associated with rivers in ancient cultures of Greece, Babylon, Egypt, and Asia and was called the “ocean stream” by Homer.

-Other Greek myths portrayed these stars as the river that flowed from Aquarius.

-The constellation Eridanus is linked to the story of Phaeton, the son of the Sun god Helios. Phaeton wanted to drive his father's chariot, and when he finally got the chance, he couldn't control it. This caused chaos, and he was struck down by Zeus and fell into the river Potamos (Eridanus). The ancient Greeks thought that the constellation represented the path Phaeton took.
